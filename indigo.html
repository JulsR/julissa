INDIGO

An idea built as an space for visual art, photography and design, Indigo represents a new community for artists around the world.

The app will embrace the culture of those who desire to exhibit their personal journeys to friends, family and even to others around the world. Indigo app is a wonderful way to discover, explore, connect and share with others in the community the same passion for the visual medias. Ready to Live . Seek . Love The app will be best known for its journals entry, a feature that allow users to post a journal entry in order to keep track of their journeys and adventures. Indigo targets the young adults of Generation Z, who desires to connect and shared their experiences via their mobiles phones and smart devices (Android, iPhone, Ipad). Indigo App offers new and unique features, in order to distinguish ourself from our competitors.

Indigo is an interactive initiative that empowers users to create, discover, and connect.

The concept behind Indigo is to built a platform where users record and share their daily activities, events and memories through photos and videos. Wireframes

The idea was to create a great user-centered experience that meet the user expectations by allowing them to easily share their moments. The concept represented similar characteristics as the top social media apps specially from apps like; VSCO and Instagram. Task flows helped when thinking through the design, before a feature was developed. Indigo is a personal challenge that I toook to learn the process of building and developing a mobile application from the very beginning. With a graphical design background and technical skills I was able to design the screen blueprints of the app, visualizing the user task flow, patterns and the structure. The goal was to built simple yet funtional high-fidelity deliverables.

Brand Identity

The research was short but effective. The best approach was to create a detailed competitor analysis that will provide insights and set priorities that will differentiate the product from the other apps. The next step was to continue by creating the mockups use for user validation.

The mockups display the visual elements and visual patterns from one screen to the other. Indigo was design not just to attract user for it's modern looks and feel, but most importantly for the way the app behaves and interact.

Interactive Prototype

After the whole process of creating the low-fidelity and high-fidelity deliverables were completed, I proceed to create an interactive prototype to test the functionalities and user flow. The prototype simulate the navigation and gestures, as well as, micro interactions and clever transitions. These were addded to fill in the gaps of one screen to the other one transition.

It's a quick and dirty way to test and validate a product before design and development.. The final product. Feedback provided by the test users, helped to regroup and iterate over the wireframes to improve the user experience. After a long and fun procress it took about 2 week to complete the Indigo app, strating from the brand identity design to the UI/UX design process.
